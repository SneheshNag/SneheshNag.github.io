Snehesh Nag

I am a musician and music technologist currently based in Atlanta, USA. I am a practitioner of North Indian classical music on the sitar and have primarily been a disciple of my father, Pt. Sugato Nag.

At the moment, I am pursuing a Masters degree in Music Technology at the Georgia Institute of Technology, and am developing creative modes of musical and visual expression.

Bio

I started learning North Indian Classical Music and the sitar from my father, sitar virtuoso Pt. Sugato Nag belonging to the sarod gharana of Senia Shahjahanpore at the age of four. I have also taken lessons from Shri Pranab Naha. I was attached to the ITC Sangeet Research Academy (SRA) as a junior scholar from 2010 to 2015 under Guru Padma Bhushan Pandit Buddhadev Das Gupta. Advanced lessons from Pt. Das Gupta continued until his passing. I am fortunate to have been featured on various newspaper reviews such as this, wherein my playing has been addressed as blending a lot of “emotion, surprise elements, and aesthetic touches” on the sitar during his recitals. I have performed to learned audiences in Kolkata, Mumbai, Pune, San Francisco, New York, Seattle, Washington, Madison, Toronto and Vancouver. I stood 2nd in the All India Radio (AIR) music competition, 2014, was was awarded the Acharya Allauddin Khan Memorial Award 2014 from Mumbai in Jan 2015 and have become a regular performer for All India Radio. I currently hold the national scholarship recognized by the Ministry of Culture, Government of India. I have also learnt the tabla from tabla maestro Pt. Tanmoy Bose and have taken vocal instruction from Shri Sandeep Nag.
