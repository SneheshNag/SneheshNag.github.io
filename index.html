Snehesh Nag

I am a musician and music technologist currently based in Atlanta, USA. I am a practitioner of North Indian classical music on the sitar and have primarily been a disciple of my father, Pt. Sugato Nag.

At the moment, I am pursuing a Masters degree in Music Technology at the Georgia Institute of Technology, where I am developing creative modes of musical and visual expression.

Bio

I started learning North Indian Classical Music and the sitar from my father, sitar virtuoso Pt. Sugato Nag, belonging to the sarod gharana of Senia Shahjahanpore at the age of four. I have also taken lessons from Shri Pranab Naha. I was attached to the ITC Sangeet Research Academy (SRA) as a junior scholar from 2010 to 2015 under Guru Padma Bhushan Pandit Buddhadev Das Gupta. Advanced lessons from Pt. Das Gupta continued until his passing. In addition to the style of the Senia Shahjahanpore gharana, I like to infuse other styles, especially the Imdadkhani gharana style, into my playing. I am fortunate to have been featured on various newspaper reviews such as The Hindu, wherein my playing has been addressed as one that blends a lot of “emotion, surprise elements, and aesthetic touches”. I have performed to learned audiences in Kolkata, Mumbai, Pune, San Francisco, New York, Seattle, Washington, Madison, Toronto and Vancouver. I stood 2nd in the All India Radio (AIR) music competition, 2014, was was awarded the Acharya Allauddin Khan Memorial Award 2014 from Mumbai in Jan 2015 and have become a regular performer for All India Radio. I currently hold the national scholarship recognized by the Ministry of Culture, Government of India. I have also learnt the tabla from tabla maestro Pt. Tanmoy Bose and have taken vocal instruction from Shri Sandeep Nag. At Georgia Tech, I am designing systems that generate audio/visuals during live performances. Most recently, I have been working on the development of an audio reactive system that takes my sitar signal as an input, processes it, and generates visuals.
